Biography

Dan Brown, born on June 22, 1964, is an American author best known for his thriller novels, including "The Da Vinci Code," "Angels & Demons," and "Inferno." His works often explore themes of cryptography, symbols, and secret societies.

Brown's novels have been translated into more than 50 languages and have sold millions of copies worldwide. His storytelling blends historical and scientific references, making his books both thrilling and intellectually engaging.
